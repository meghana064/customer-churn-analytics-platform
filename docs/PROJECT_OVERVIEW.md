# Project Overview — Customer Churn Prediction

## What is Customer Churn?
**Customer churn** refers to customers stopping their relationship with a business. Depending on the industry, churn can mean:
- Canceling a subscription (SaaS, streaming)
- Switching telecom providers
- Closing an account (banking/fintech)
- Not returning to purchase again (retail)

Churn is commonly represented as a binary target variable such as `Churn = Yes/No`.

## Why do Businesses Predict Churn?
Predicting churn helps organizations move from reacting after customers leave to acting **before** they leave. With churn prediction, businesses can:
- Identify at-risk customers early
- Prioritize retention outreach (e.g., offers, support, upgrades)
- Improve customer experience by targeting pain points
- Reduce revenue loss and acquisition pressure

## Business Value
Churn prediction delivers measurable value by enabling:
- **Higher retention**: Retention campaigns can be targeted instead of broad and costly.
- **Lower costs**: Acquiring new customers is often more expensive than retaining existing ones.
- **Revenue protection**: Reduces recurring revenue churn and improves lifetime value (LTV).
- **Smarter decision-making**: Data-driven insights into factors that correlate with churn.

## Expected Outcome
The expected output of this project is a workflow that can:
- Train a churn classifier on historical customer data
- Generate churn probabilities for new customers
- Support business actions such as segmentation and retention strategies
